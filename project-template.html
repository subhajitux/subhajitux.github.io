Optimising Pharmacy Prescription Flow

UX Design ‚Ä¢ Research ‚Ä¢ Jan 2024

The Challenge

Users were dropping off at the prescription upload stage at a rate of 15%. Our goal was to understand the friction points and redesign the flow to be seamless, trustworthy, and efficient.

We needed to balance legal compliance (uploading valid RX) with user ease-of-use.

Auditing the Old Flow

The previous design felt cluttered. Users weren't sure if they needed to upload a document immediately or if they could consult a doctor later.

We identified 3 major pain points:

  • Confusing copy
  • Too many steps
  • Lack of visual hierarchy

In-depth Analysis

Click on the items below to see more details about our user research and technical constraints.

We conducted 12 moderated usability tests. Key findings included that users felt anxious about privacy and didn't realize they could use a gallery image.

The OCR system required high-contrast images. This led to the implementation of a "Real-time Quality Check" feature in the camera UI.

Explorations & Wireframes

We started with low-fidelity sketches to map out the new user journey.

The Final Solution

We introduced a bottom-sheet architecture that allowed users to maintain context of their cart while uploading documents.

The new design is cleaner, uses reassuring copy, and clearly distinguishes between "I have a prescription" and "I need a consultation".

The Impact

After A/B testing the new flow for 3 weeks, we observed:

+12%

Conversion Rate

-4%

Drop-off Rate
